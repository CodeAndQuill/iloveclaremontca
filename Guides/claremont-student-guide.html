Student Guide

Welcome to Claremont!

Everything outside the orientation packet.

You may not know it yet, but our City of Trees and PhDs is pretty spectacular — it’s a small, walkable city with a genuine personality. Here’s an off-campus guide that may not have been included at orientation.

The Lay of the Land

Claremont is easy to navigate once you understand that the city runs in three horizontal bands, stacked south to north. The colleges sit at the bottom of the middle band.

🏠

The Village — south, bordering campus

Your walkable downtown along Indian Hill Blvd — restaurants, coffee, the museum, the train station, and the Sunday Farmers Market. Most of your off-campus time lands here.

🛒

The Foothill Strip — ~1 mile north

Trader Joe’s, Stater Bros, CVS — everything practical. Straight shot up Yale Ave, 15 minutes on foot or 5 by bike.

🏕️

The Foothills — where the mountains start

Claremont Hills Wilderness Park, Potato Mountain, California Botanic Garden — all within a short bike ride. Don’t wait until senior year.

Shipping & Returns

If the campus mailroom is closed and you need to ship something immediately, handle a return, or just buy stamps, here are your off-campus options in Claremont.

USPS · In the Village

Claremont Post Office

140 Harvard Ave. Stamps, certified mail, passport applications, flat-rate boxes. Weekday mornings — check USPS.com for hours before you go.

UPS Store · 310 N. Indian Hill Blvd

The UPS Store

Amazon QR returns (they supply the box), packaging, and printing. Mon–Fri 8:30am–6:30pm, Sat 9am–5pm.

Groceries Near Campus

Everything you need for day-to-day survival is on or near Foothill Blvd, accessible on foot or by bike about a mile north of campus. Here is what’s near:

475 W. Foothill Blvd

Trader Joe’s

The student staple. Go weekday mornings — weekends are a zoo.

1055 W. Foothill Blvd · Open 6am–11pm daily

Stater Bros

Full supermarket, half a mile from TJ’s. Better for brand names, a real deli counter, and anything Trader Joe’s doesn’t carry.

Also on Foothill Blvd

CVS

Cold medicine at 11pm, prescriptions, toiletries. Same errand run as Trader Joe’s. Hours →

The Claremont Sunday Farmers Market

Every Sunday · 8am–1pm · Second St & Harvard Ave, the Village

Claremont Farmers & Artisans Market

~60 vendors: fresh fruit, pupusas, pastries, honey, cut flowers. Walk down Sunday morning, grab breakfast from a vendor, do a lap. Free to browse — you’ll go every week.

Banking & ATMs

Most major national banks have branches within walking distance, but check your bank’s app for the nearest ATM before making a special trip.

The Village · Indian Hill Blvd

Village Branches

Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, California Bank & Trust, and First City Credit Union — all walkable from campus along Indian Hill Blvd.

Foothill Blvd & Indian Hill

Foothill Branches

US Bank and Banc of California at the Foothill and Indian Hill intersection — same errand run as Trader Joe’s.

🎉 Your secret weapon: the Foothill Transit Class Pass

All eight Claremont Colleges get free Foothill Transit. Pick up a TAP card at the Connection, or use the TAP app. Activate within 14 days. Covers all Foothill Transit buses — six lines serve campus directly (188, 197, 292, 480, 492, 690); Line 188 runs along Indian Hill Blvd daily. Lost card replacement is $10. Full details →

🌡️ It will be hotter than you expect — bring a fan

August and September hit 100°F+ regularly, and the heat doesn’t break until November. Whatever your housing packet says about AC, bring a fan anyway. Take Line 188 on your TAP card east to Target in Montclair — fans, extension cords, bedding, whatever you forgot. 15 minutes, free.
