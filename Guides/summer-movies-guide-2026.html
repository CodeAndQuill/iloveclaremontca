🎬 Free Summer Movies in the Park

A five-week, five-district lineup of free family movie nights across Claremont — the 2026 schedule, showtimes, and how to nail your picnic setup.

There is nothing quite like spreading a picnic blanket on the grass and watching a great film under the stars. Claremont's beloved Movies in the Park series is officially back for summer 2026, and it features a fantastic five-week lineup designed to bring free family entertainment to every corner of town. This year, the city is bringing the cinematic fun straight to your neighborhood by hosting a different screening across all five of Claremont's city districts. The series kicked off Wednesday, July 8 with UP at Lewis Park (District 1) — below is your ultimate playbook for the four remaining nights.

Movie-night reminders, Claremont summer events, and local alerts — one email, only when there's something worth showing up for.

⏰ The Standard Movie Night Timeline

Every screening except the National Night Out finale follows the same evening rhythm:

7:30 PM · Free Kids' Pre-Show Activities

Arrive early. The city hosts free kid-friendly games, crafts, and themed activities to keep the little ones entertained while the sun goes down.

Dusk (approx. 8:15 PM) · Showtime

The film starts as soon as it is dark enough for the projector screen — usually right around 8:15–8:30 PM in mid-summer.

📅 The Official 2026 Movie & Location Lineup

Four screenings remain for summer 2026 — each in a different Claremont district.

Thu, July 16

June Vail Park (Grand Ave & Bluefield Dr) · District 4

Matilda 📚

A '90s classic that never gets old. Bring the family to watch the extraordinary little girl use her brilliant mind (and a touch of telekinesis) to take on the formidable Miss Trunchbull.

Wed, July 22

Wheeler Park (626 Vista Dr) · District 2

The Wild Robot 🤖

Missed this gorgeous animated film in theaters last fall? Now is your chance to catch the touching story of Roz, a shipwrecked robot who learns to adapt to a remote island and becomes the adoptive parent to an orphaned gosling.

Thu, July 30

Memorial Park (840 N. Indian Hill Blvd) · District 3

How to Train Your Dragon 🐉

High-flying Viking adventure comes to our central park. Perfect for older kids and anyone who loves spectacular animation and a phenomenal orchestral score.

Tue, August 4 · National Night Out

Joāt Park (1151 N. Indian Hill Blvd) · District 5

Zootopia 2 🦊🐰

Special 6:00 PM start. The Claremont Police Department hosts its annual National Night Out celebration right on the park grounds. Starting at 6:00 PM, kids can explore real CPD police vehicles, browse interactive community booths, play games, and win prizes. The movie follows at dusk.

🧺 Pro Tips for a Perfect Movie Night

To keep you and your neighbors comfortable, keep a few local park ground rules in mind:

Low Chairs are King

If you are bringing lawn chairs, please make sure they are low-backed beach-style chairs. High-backed camping chairs easily block the screen for families sitting on blankets behind you.

Pack an Extra Layer

Even when the day is scorching, the foothill breeze can make our parks surprisingly crisp after 9:00 PM. Throw a few extra sweatshirts or a thick stadium blanket into your wagon just in case.

Leave the Pups at Home

While we love our furry friends, dogs are strictly prohibited in most of these city parks (including Memorial, Lewis, and Joāt). Leave them cozy at home where they won't be startled by loud movie audio systems.

Strict Park Ordinances

All Claremont public parks are completely alcohol-free and smoke-free zones.

Wildlife Awareness

Before you lay your blanket down near the park tree-lines or shrubbery at twilight, take a quick glance at our live Claremont Coyote Sighting Map to check recent neighborhood wildlife activity.

One More Thing

Weather or scheduling questions? For last-minute updates or weather delay information, check the City of Claremont's official Movies in the Park page.
