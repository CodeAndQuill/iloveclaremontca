The 91711 Election Guide

Everything you need to know about the April 28 gubernatorial debate at Pomona College

Date April 28, 2026
Venue Bridges Auditorium
Broadcast CBS News California
Debate Starts 5:30 PM

On April 28, Pomona College's iconic Bridges Auditorium will host the 2026 California gubernatorial debate — broadcast live statewide on CBS News. Here's everything Claremont residents need to know to follow, participate, and make the most of debate night.

1. The Ticket Reality Check

Limited Seating — Here's the Deal

Bridges Auditorium (Big Bridges) is a massive venue, but for this event, it is a "closed production." Between the multiple camera rigs, lighting grids, and heightened security, physical seating is extremely limited.

  • Priority Seating: Seating is being prioritized for Pomona College students, faculty, and staff via an internal lottery.
  • Community Access: While there may be a very small number of seats released to the broader Claremont community, those details are still being finalized. Monitor the official Pomona Debate Page for any "Get Involved" updates.
The Heroic Tip

Don't waste your evening hunting for tickets on the secondary market — tickets are non-transferable and require a photo ID matching the reservation.

2. The 91711 Watch Party Kit

Since the auditorium is essentially a TV studio for the night, the Associated Students of Pomona College (ASPC) and the League of Women Voters are creating the city's "public square" at Frary Dining Hall.

Where Frary Dining Hall (347 E. Sixth St.) Coverage Starts 5:00 PM Debate Begins 5:30 PM

What to expect:

  • Live Media Presence: CBS News correspondents will be on-site to capture live community reactions for the broadcast.
  • Interactive Elements: Expect live digital polls and reaction threads where you can weigh in on candidate performance in real-time.
  • Expert Analysis: Stick around after the 90-minute debate for continued programming featuring faculty experts and special guests who will break down the night's biggest moments.

3. Logistics & Debate Night Strategy

Expect a security perimeter to develop around the Southeast Village starting at noon on the 28th.

Date & Time Tuesday, April 28, 2026
Broadcasting CBS News California (Live Statewide)
Moderators Prof. Sara Sadhwani & CBS News Journalists
Public Parking Use the 1st Street Structure or the Metrolink Lot
Protest Zone North side of Marston Quad (Official Free Speech Zone)

4. Frequently Asked Questions

Can I watch from home?

Yes. The debate will be broadcast live on all CBS stations statewide and streamed via their digital platforms. If you are a commuter, tune in to KSPC 88.7 FM for the local radio feed.

What is the "Free Speech Zone"?

The college has designated Marston Quad as the official area for demonstrations and signage. This ensures candidate motorcades see the messages while keeping emergency access lanes clear on College Way.

When do we actually vote?

Primary election ballots are scheduled to be mailed to California voters just five days after the debate (May 3). This makes the April 28 event the final "deep dive" before we head to the polls.
