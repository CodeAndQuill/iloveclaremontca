Pilgrim Place First Saturday Sales

How to score the best vintage finds — and how to donate your own — at Claremont's first-Saturday sale

When First Saturday of the Month
Time 9:00 AM – 11:00 AM
Season April – September
Rating 4.7 out of 5
Proceeds 100% → Resident Health & Support Program

If you've lived in Claremont for more than five minutes, you know the annual Pilgrim Place Festival in November is legendary. What many locals don't realize: you don't have to wait until autumn to experience the magic. On the first Saturday of the month from April through September, this storied retirement community opens its historic campus gates for a fast-paced, highly curated neighborhood market — and because the residents here include retired professors, clergy, and professionals, the items are frequently of a caliber you won't find at a standard yard sale.

2. What You'll Find: The "Big Four" Stops

The Furniture Barn

This is the destination for solid wood dining sets, mid-century modern desks, and unique patio furniture. If you see it, stay with it — the Furniture Barn is the most competitive area of the sale.

The Boutique & Household Goods

A goldmine for high-quality linens, silver-plate, and unique ceramics. This is where you'll find those "Claremont-style" decor pieces that give a home character.

The Book Room

Given our proximity to the Colleges, the book selection here is unparalleled. Expect rare academic texts, vintage hardbacks, and niche collections you won't find at a big-box bookstore.

Fine Art & Vintage Treasures

Original paintings, framed prints, signed lithographs, and travel keepsakes from residents whose decades-long careers took them around the world. The one-of-a-kind pieces here come with a story — and a Pilgrim Place sale is one of the few places you might overhear a docent-level explanation of what you're looking at.

3. How to Donate

The "First Saturday Sales" are a beloved Claremont tradition, but they only work because of the constant cycle of high-quality donations from the community. If you have items that are "too good for a dumpster but taking up too much room," here is how to get them to Pilgrim Place.

What They Accept

  • Furniture: Clean, sturdy pieces (no broken frames or major stains)
  • Collectibles: Jewelry, art, and high-end boutique items
  • Household Items: Working small appliances, kitchenware, and linens
  • Books: Clean copies (no water damage or excessive highlighting)

How to Drop Off

  • Where: Donations are generally accepted at the Town Center area (near the Avery Road entrance)
  • Contact First: Always call the Pilgrim Place Front Desk before dropping off large furniture or bulk donations. They can confirm if they have space in the "Barn" and provide you with a tax-deductible receipt.
  • Timing: Do not drop off items on the morning of a sale! The best time to donate is during weekday business hours when staff and resident volunteers are available to help.

4. The "Pro" Logistics

Parking

Parking inside the Pilgrim Place grounds is strictly prohibited during sales. Park on Avery Road or Harrison Avenue, but be mindful of driveways — the local parking enforcement is very active on sale mornings.

The "Wagon" Rule

If you plan on buying more than a few books or kitchen items, bring a folding wagon. You will likely have to walk a block or two back to your car with your finds.

Payment

Cash is the fastest way to check out. Some larger stations may take credit cards, but having cash ready ensures you don't lose out on a find while waiting for a signal.

Why These Sales Matter

100% of the proceeds from these sales go to the Resident Health and Support Program, which ensures that residents who outlive their financial resources can remain in their homes. When you buy a vintage lamp or donate your old bookshelf, you are directly supporting the seniors who make Claremont so special.
