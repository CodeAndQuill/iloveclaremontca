How this map works

Anyone in Claremont can drop a pin to report a coyote they've seen. Pins stay visible for 72 hours from when the sighting happened, then fade off the map. Each pin is snapped to the nearest block before it's saved — that way, the map shows you the general area without revealing exactly where the submitter was standing.

The goal is a low-stakes neighborhood awareness layer, not an emergency reporting system. If a coyote is sick, injured, or acting aggressively, please call IVHS (909) 623-9777 in addition to logging the pin here. For a full breakdown of what to do during and after an encounter, see the Claremont Coyote Encounter Guide.
