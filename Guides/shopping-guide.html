Claremont Retail Therapy

A Local’s Guide to Village Shopping

The Village isn't a mall — it's a neighborhood that happens to have incredible shops. Whether you're a local or visiting for the weekend, park once, walk everywhere, and let the tree-lined blocks surprise you.

Phase 1: The Wardrobe Pillars

When you need to move beyond campus casual and into something more professional or sophisticated, these boutiques anchor the Claremont fashion scene.

Amelie

Located on Yale Avenue, this is the gold standard for chic, contemporary women's fashion. It favors clean lines over bohemian layers — high-quality fabrics, sharp denim, and modern silhouettes. The definitive spot for a power blazer or a staple that actually lasts.

Maison Garrison

For a European-inspired aesthetic, Maison Garrison is unmatched. This woman-owned boutique focuses on sophisticated silhouettes and timeless investment pieces. If you want a look that says "executive editor" rather than "music festival," this is your headquarters.

Susa Boutique

The destination for elegant and timeless fashion. If you find the other boutiques a bit too trendy, Susa offers a more classic, upscale collection that focuses on quality construction and sophisticated, clean lines.

Phase 2: Specialty Finds & Gifts

For the home chef, the design lover, or the person who always finds the perfect gift. These shops prioritize high design and unique inventory.

The Hens' Kitchen Shoppe

A culinary paradise that feels like a professional toolkit. High-end kitchen tools paired with classic home decor. Whether you're a serious baker or just looking for a standout housewarming gift, this is a Village essential.

The Grove Home

Beautifully curated home goods, stationery, and stylish accessories like BAGGU totes. A playful but polished mix of decor and gifts — the kind of shop where you walk in for one thing and leave with a bag full.

A Shop Called Quest

Tucked into the Packing House, this is a vibrant haven for graphic novels, art books, and pop culture collectibles. Appeals to everyone from hardcore collectors to casual fans who appreciate well-curated shelves.

Phase 3: Vintage & Independent Spirit

Claremont's alternative side — with a sharper, more curated edge.

Replay Vintage

Located in the Packing House, this is the premier spot for high-quality, curated vintage and retro fashion. Think 50s-through-90s staples that have been perfectly preserved and styled — more urban edge than thrift-store dig.

The Giving

A purpose-driven boutique where every purchase supports a cause. The aesthetic is clean and ethical, featuring sustainable brands and artisan-made goods that feel thoughtful and well-made.

DeeLux

The premier buy-sell-trade spot in town. A family-owned staple that mixes vintage and modern finds with an authentic edge. Sustainable shopping at its best — perfect for finding a one-of-a-kind leather jacket.

Phase 4: The Insider Playbook

The "Village Loop": These shops are densely packed. Park near the Packing House or on Yale Ave, and every store on this list is within a five-to-ten minute walk.

The reward: Because these boutiques are so close together, you can easily rack up your steps hopping between phases — the perfect excuse to justify that splurge brunch at Bardot or Union on Yale afterward.
