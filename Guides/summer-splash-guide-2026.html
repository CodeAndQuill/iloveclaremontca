The Claremont Summer Splash Guide

Wading Pools & Twilight Swims (2026) — the parent's playbook for keeping the kids cool all summer.

There is a distinct moment every June when the Inland Empire heat officially settles in, and local parents collectively face the same daily question: How are we going to burn off this energy without melting? While the Claremont pools are fantastic, sometimes you just need a quick, casual, low-stress way for toddlers and young kids to splash around. Enter Claremont's seasonal wading pools. From Monday, June 15 through Saturday, August 18, 2026, the city's neighborhood parks transform into water-play hubs. Whether you're looking for a quick mid-day splash or want to hit the festive themed Twilight Swims, here is your complete local guide to navigating the 2026 summer swim schedule.

Twilight Swim themes, pool day reminders, and summer family events — one email, only when there's something worth showing up for.

Phase 1: The Daily Wading Pool Rotation (June 15 – August 8)

For the peak weeks of summer, three of our favorite neighborhood parks open their shallow wading pools. They're free, open to the community, and run exactly from 11:00 AM to 1:00 PM — the perfect window right before afternoon naptime.

Memorial Park

The ultimate central hub. Grab a shady spot under the old-growth elms, splash in the wading pool, and let the kids dry off on the playground without ever moving the stroller.

Wheeler Park

Great for families on the north side of town who want a slightly quieter, more neighborhood-park feel. Pickleball courts and shaded picnic tables nearby for older siblings.

El Barrio Park

A fantastic spot with plenty of local history, open green space, and a dedicated neighborhood vibe. The smallest of the three pools, which often means the shortest waits.

⚠️ Important 2026 holiday closures: Double-check your calendar before loading up the strollers.
  • Friday, June 19 (Juneteenth): All pools closed.
  • Thursday, July 2 – Friday, July 3: Memorial Park pool closed.
  • Saturday, July 4 (Independence Day): El Barrio Park & Wheeler Park pools closed.

Phase 2: The 2026 Twilight Swims 🌙

If you want to beat the midday sun entirely, the city is hosting six themed evening swims from 5:00 to 7:00 PM. These are high-energy community events — perfect for packing a picnic dinner, meeting up with neighbors, and enjoying the cooler twilight air.

Monday, June 22

World Cup Night — Memorial Park

Kick off the twilight season with a soccer-themed splash right in the heart of town.

Wednesday, July 1

Western Night — Wheeler Park

Dust off the cowboy hats for a festive country-and-western theme on the north side.

Tuesday, July 14

Under the Sea — El Barrio Park

The quintessential ocean-themed pool night — perfect for your littlest swimmers.

Thursday, July 23

K-Pop Night — Memorial Park

High energy, great music, and a festive atmosphere for the middle of summer.

Tuesday, July 28

Space Night — Wheeler Park

An out-of-this-world evening swim under the stars.

Wednesday, August 5

Glow Night — El Barrio Park

Close out the themed series with glow sticks and neon summer fun.

Phase 3: Planning a Party? (The Official Paperwork)

You can actually reserve these park picnic areas and wading pools for private events, like summer birthday parties. If you're looking to host a backyard-style bash without destroying your own lawn, the City of Claremont makes it incredibly easy — provided you fill out the right paperwork in advance.

1. Check Availability

Visit the official Claremont Park & Wading Pool Reservations portal to check your dates before locking in anything else.

2. Read the Rules

Read the official City of Claremont Park Picnic and Wading Pool Rules & Regulations (PDF) to make sure your party favors, decorations, and setups are compliant.

3. Submit the Application

Download the official Park Picnic Area and Wading Pool Reservation Application (PDF) and submit it directly to the city.

Pro-Tips for Claremont Parents

The 1:00 PM exit: The wading pools close promptly at 1:00 PM. Have your towels and post-swim snacks ready to go before the whistle blows so you can make a seamless transition to the car without the mid-day meltdown.

Shade strategy: While our parks are beautifully tree-lined, the immediate concrete perimeters around the wading pools get hot. Bring water shoes for the little ones and a sturdy pop-up sun shade if you plan to stay the full two hours.

Got questions? If the weather looks weird or you want to double-check pool conditions, call our friends at the Alexander Hughes Community Center at (909) 399-5490. They run the recreation logistics and are always incredibly helpful.
