Claremont Summer Concerts in the Park

Free Monday nights at the Memorial Park bandshell through August 24, 2026. The full lineup, parking, and picnic playbook.

There is nothing quite like a warm twilight evening in Claremont when the community gathers under the mature shade trees of Memorial Park. The city's annual Monday Night Concerts are a beloved local tradition — free, family-friendly outdoor shows every Monday night from July 6 through August 24, 2026. The lineup runs the gamut from 70's Top 40 and country to reggae, a Beatles tribute, and classic rock — the perfect excuse to pack a picnic blanket, round up the neighbors, and enjoy live music in the heart of town. Presented by the City of Claremont and the Kiwanis Club of Claremont.

Concert lineup updates, Claremont summer events, and local alerts — one email, only when there's something worth showing up for.

🫘 The Picnic Playbook: Snacks & Setup

The park lawn fills up well before the first downbeat at 7:00 PM. A few local tips to make your Monday evening seamless:

Spot Scouting

For prime late-afternoon shade before the show starts, look for spots on the east side of the lawn under the historic oaks.

Chairs vs. Blankets

High-backed lawn chairs are best placed toward the perimeter so they don't block the view of neighbors on blankets closer to the stage.

Pack a Layer

Even on hot summer days, the foothill breeze cools the park down quickly after dusk — a light jacket or sweater goes a long way.

On-Site Eats

The Claremont Kiwanis Club typically runs a concession stand at the concerts to benefit local youth programs — bring some cash just in case.

🚗 Parking, Transit & Getting There

Memorial Park is centrally located just north of the Village, and streets fill up fast on Monday nights.

Street Parking

Available along Indian Hill Blvd, Harrison Ave, and Yale Ave — but it goes early.

Better Idea: Park & Stroll

Park in one of the public structures in the Village and take a beautiful, short walk north through the tree-lined historic avenues.

Bike It

The park is an easy bike ride from anywhere central to Claremont. Check the city's event page closer to showtime to see if a bike valet is being offered this season.

🐕 A Note on Pets & Wildlife

Memorial Park is normally a haven for local dogs, but summer concerts get crowded and loud.

Pet Safety

If your dog is sensitive to crowds, loud speakers, or applause, leave them resting safely at home. If you do bring your pup, they must remain on a secure leash at all times.

Wildlife Awareness

Don't forget to check our Claremont Coyote Sighting Map before walking to and from the park — wildlife frequently crosses the local greenways during twilight hours.

Pro-Tips for Claremont Concert Nights

Arrive a little early: The lawn fills up between 6:00 and 6:45 PM. Showing up around 6:00 PM gives you time to claim a good spot, lay out the blanket, and settle in before the first downbeat.

Cash for concessions: If you want to skip the cooler prep, bring small bills for the Kiwanis stand — your dollars also support local youth programs.
