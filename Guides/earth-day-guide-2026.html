The 2026 Claremont Earth Day Guide

Plants, puppets, and practical green living — how to navigate the city's biggest environmental event without the parking headache

Date April 22, 2026
Location Shelton Park
Organized By Sustainable Claremont
Cost Free

For a city that prides itself on being a "City With Trees and PhDs," Earth Day isn't just a date on the calendar — it's the Super Bowl of local sustainability. Organized by Sustainable Claremont, the 2026 celebration is shaping up to be a high-impact morning of education and action.

As a resident or local researcher, you know that navigating Village events during the Sunday Farmers Market requires a bit of tactical planning. Here is your definitive guide to making the most of the festivities at Shelton Park.

What to Expect

Native Plant Giveaways

One of the most popular draws every year. Sustainable Claremont partners with local nurseries to distribute free native plants to attendees — California poppies, sage, buckwheat, and other drought-tolerant species perfect for the 91711 climate. These tend to go fast, so arrive early if you want the best selection.

Puppet Shows & Kids' Activities

Earth Day in Claremont has always been family-friendly. Expect puppet shows with environmental themes, hands-on craft stations using recycled materials, and nature scavenger hunts through the park. This is one of the best free kids' events in the Inland Empire.

Practical Green Living Workshops

Beyond the festivities, this is where the event earns its "PhD" credentials. Expect workshops and demos on:

  • Composting at Home: How to turn your kitchen scraps into garden gold
  • Water-Wise Landscaping: Practical tips for drought-tolerant yards
  • Solar & Energy Efficiency: Local experts break down rebates and incentives
  • Zero-Waste Living: Small changes that actually make a difference

Community Booths & Local Organizations

Dozens of local nonprofits, environmental groups, and city departments set up booths throughout the park. This is your chance to connect with the people doing the work — from the Claremont Wildlands Conservancy to the city's own sustainability team.

The Parking Strategy

Earth Day falls on a weekday this year, but Shelton Park is right in the heart of the Village. Here's how to avoid the headache:

  • 1st Street Parking Structure: Your best bet. Free and just a short walk to the park.
  • Metrolink Lot: Plenty of overflow parking, especially if you arrive after 9 AM.
  • Street Parking: Available on side streets, but read the signs carefully — some have 2-hour limits that are enforced.
  • Bike or Walk: If you live nearby, this is the day to practice what we preach. Bike racks are available at the park.

Arrive by 9 AM for the best parking and the first pick of native plants.

Pro Tips

Bring Your Own Bag

You'll want something to carry home your free plants, pamphlets, and any goodies from the booths. A reusable tote is on-brand and practical.

Combine It with the Farmers Market

If Earth Day falls on a Sunday, the Claremont Farmers Market will be running simultaneously just blocks away. Make a morning of it — sustainability education followed by locally grown produce.

Sunscreen & Water

April in Claremont can surprise you. Even a morning event at the park can get warm. Bring a refillable water bottle and sunscreen — the shade trees help, but the open lawn areas can be toasty.

Why It Matters

Claremont was one of the first cities in the region to adopt a Climate Action Plan, and events like Earth Day are where that plan meets the community. Whether you're picking up a free California buckwheat for your front yard or learning how to cut your water bill in half, every small action adds up. This is the 91711 at its best — neighbors learning together how to take care of the place we all love.
