Taste of Claremont Turns 25

The Rotary Club's milestone fundraiser returns to the Village on April 18

Date Saturday, April 18, 2026
Where TCCS · 101 S. Mills Ave
Age 21+ only
Get Tickets →

For a quarter century, the Rotary Club of Claremont has been hosting one of the Village's signature community events — and this Saturday marks a milestone. On April 18, 2026, the 25th annual Taste of Claremont takes over the grounds of The Claremont Colleges Services, bringing together local restaurants, wineries, and breweries for an evening that funds a full year of Rotary's community work.

The basics

  • Date: Saturday, April 18, 2026
  • Location: The Claremont Colleges Services (TCCS), 101 South Mills Avenue
  • Age requirement: 21 and over only, strictly enforced
  • Format: A ticketed evening of tastings from local restaurants, wineries, and breweries
  • Raffle: Over $10,000 in prizes, benefiting the Rotary Club
  • Tickets: Available through the official event site

Start and end times, along with the full vendor lineup, are available through the official event page and the Eventbrite listing. Tickets for past years have sold well in the final week leading up to the event, so attendees typically plan ahead rather than counting on walk-up availability.

Where your ticket goes

Taste of Claremont isn't a food festival that happens to donate proceeds — it is the Rotary Club of Claremont's annual fundraiser. Every ticket, every raffle purchase, and every sponsorship funds the Rotary's year-round community work.

About the Rotary Club of Claremont

Rotary is one of the oldest and most respected civic service organizations in the world. The Claremont chapter supports scholarships for local students, youth enrichment programs, community health initiatives, and international humanitarian projects.

When you buy a ticket, a meaningful portion is a charitable contribution to that work. Taste of Claremont is, in practical terms, the single biggest night of fundraising the local Rotary does each year.

If you're going

  • Bring a physical ID. The 21+ policy is strict and no exceptions are made at the gate.
  • Wear flat, comfortable shoes. The TCCS grounds are a mix of pavement and grass, and the event runs for several hours.
  • Plan your transportation. For the wine and spirits portion of the evening, a rideshare or Claremont Dial-a-Ride is the safer option.
  • Buy tickets in advance. Walk-up availability isn't guaranteed, and past years have sold out.

Twenty-five years in, Taste of Claremont has become a Village tradition — a night where local hospitality, community service, and small-town spirit come together for a cause that keeps giving back long after the evening ends.
