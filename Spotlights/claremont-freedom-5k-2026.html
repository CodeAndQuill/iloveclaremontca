The Local Runner's Guide to the 2026 Claremont Freedom 5K

Earn your morning pancakes on a new reverse-direction course — here's the full race-day playbook.

Date Saturday, July 4, 2026
Start Memorial Park · N. Yale Ave
Kids 1K 7:30 AM
5K 8:00 AM
Register & Race Info →

There is no better way to kick off the Fourth of July in the 91711 than earning your morning pancakes at the annual Claremont Freedom 5K (and 1K Kids Fun Run). Hosted by the Claremont Sunrise Rotary Club and the Claremont Cross Country Program, this neighborhood favorite draws hundreds of local runners, families, and walkers to Memorial Park. Whether you are aiming for an age-group medal or pacing your kids on their first official loop, here's the race-day playbook and course-strategy guide.

📅Essential Race-Day Logistics

Everything starts and finishes at Memorial Park (840 N. Indian Hill Blvd), on N. Yale Ave along the east side of the park.

Race Morning Schedule

Saturday, July 4, 2026 · Memorial Park

  • 6:00 – 7:30 AMRace-morning packet pickup & registration — grab your bib on the east side of the park.
  • 7:30 AM1K Kids Fun Run/Walk steps off.
  • 7:30 AMRegistration & corrals close — strictly.
  • 8:00 AMFreedom 5K starts.
⚠ Crucial arrival warning: Race registration and the starting corrals close strictly at 7:30 AM on race morning. Plan to arrive at least 30–45 minutes early to secure parking, stretch, and use the restrooms. Organizers recommend parking on the west side of Indian Hill Blvd to avoid course closures.

🏉Packet Pickup Windows

Three chances to grab your bib number and official race shirt.

Where & When to Get Your Bib

Three options across three days

  • Thu, July 24:30 – 7:00 PM at Claremont Craft Ales (1420 N. Claremont Blvd #204c). Swing by early, grab your bib, and kick off the holiday weekend with a local cold brew. Tip: park on Andrew Drive for this window.
  • Fri, July 34:30 – 7:00 PM at Memorial Park — pick it up right next to the starting line the evening before.
  • Sat, July 46:00 – 7:30 AM at Memorial Park — available for early risers, but lines can get long!

🗺Course Strategy & Crucial Change for 2026

If you have run this race in years past, take note: there is a new route for 2026. The Freedom 5K is utilizing the official Claremont Turkey Trot course map, but running it completely in reverse. While the course stays on familiar paved neighborhood roads around the Village and the Colleges, the pacing math shifts — so plan accordingly.

Two aid stations are on course: Mills Ave around Mile 1.2 and College Ave around Mile 2.2. Know where they are before you line up.

🏃How to Pace It Like a Pro

The First Half: Fast & Flowing

Because the course is running the Turkey Trot in reverse, the early miles trend easier than the finish. It is incredibly easy to get caught up in the race-day adrenaline and blow past your target pace here. Keep your ego in check and lock into a smooth, sustainable rhythm.

The Second Half: The College Ave Climb

This is where patience early on pays dividends. The final stretch back toward Memorial Park includes a steady, grinding incline as you make your way up College Avenue. Save an extra gear in your tank to power through the gentle foothill climb to the finish line.

Lining Up Safely

For everyone's safety, the first few rows of the N. Yale Ave starting gate are reserved for competitive runners holding a 7:00/mile pace or faster. Walkers, strollers, and casual joggers should line up toward the middle and back of the pack.

☀Heat, Hydration & Prep Tips

Running a 5K in early July along the San Gabriel foothills means dealing with summer heat and climbing humidity. A few local tips:

  • Pre-hydrate starting today. Don't try to chug a gallon of water on race morning — it will just bounce around in your stomach. Start increasing your water intake now so your muscles are fully hydrated by Saturday.
  • Warm up wisely. Spend 5–10 minutes doing dynamic stretches (butt kicks, leg swings, walking lunges) in the shade of Memorial Park before lining up. Avoid static stretching on cold muscles.
  • The post-race pivot. The second you cross the finish line and collect your medal, keep walking to clear the lactic acid. Then pivot straight to the Kiwanis Club Pancake Breakfast (7–10 AM) inside the park to refuel.
  • Stay for the whole day. The morning festival, flag raising, and 1 PM parade all launch from Memorial Park — make it a full Claremont Fourth.

🔗Register & Track Results

For final registration details or to check live results on race day, bookmark the official Claremont Freedom 5K RunSignup page. And if you're already looking ahead to the rest of the day, we've got the full 2026 Claremont Fourth of July playbook too — parade route, fireworks tickets, everything.

One more heads-up: if you are planning any final independent training runs around the foothill greenways this week, keep an eye on our live Claremont Coyote Sighting Map. Have a fast, safe race, Claremont.
