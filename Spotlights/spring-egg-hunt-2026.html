The 91711 Parent's Guide to the 2026 Spring Egg Hunt

How to actually score an egg — and a pancake — at Memorial Park.

In collaboration with the City of Claremont & the Children's Foundation of America
When Saturday, April 4, 2026
Time 9:00 AM – 11:00 AM
Where Memorial Park
Cost Free (Breakfast $8)
Event Details

The egg hunt is FREE. The pancake breakfast is $8. The whole thing is over by 11 AM. Here's how to win the morning.

The Spring Egg Hunt at Memorial Park is back for 2026, and it's more than just a hunt — petting zoo, face painting, the Kiwanis Pancake Breakfast, and the quest for the golden egg. But between the 10:45 AM rush and the parking chaos, having a plan is the difference between a great morning and a meltdown in the grass.

The Logistics: A Timeline for Success

To make the most of your morning, you need to treat the schedule like a flight itinerary. The event runs from 9:00 AM to 11:00 AM, but the "hunt" itself is a flash in the pan.

8:00 AM – 11:00 AM

The Fuel

The Kiwanis Club of Claremont hosts their famous pancake breakfast. It's $8 per person, and it's the best way to secure a parking spot before the crowds arrive.

9:00 AM

The Sensory-Friendly Start

The City hosts a Special Needs Egg Hunt at 9:00 AM sharp. This is an adaptive, sensory-friendly hunt where parents are encouraged to assist their children.

While You Wait: The “Secret” History

Between breakfast and the hunt, take a walk by the Garner House located right in the park. It's the home of Claremont Heritage, and in honor of their 50th Anniversary, it's a great chance to show your kids the 1926 Spanish Colonial masterpiece that serves as the heart of our city's history.

10:45 AM

The Main Event

The traditional egg hunt for children ages 3–12 begins promptly. If you arrive at 10:45, you've already missed it. You'll want to be at the perimeter of the field by 10:30 AM at the latest.

Age-Group Game Plan

Because the needs of a one-year-old are vastly different from those of a fifth-grader, we've broken down the best way to navigate the morning based on your child's age.

Ages 2 & Under

For the Toddlers

For the youngest Claremont residents, the main field can be overwhelming. The city has designed a low-stress alternative for this group.

Goodie Bag Pickup: Instead of a competitive hunt, children ages 2 and younger can collect free goodie bags at the designated booths.

Petting Zoo Priority: Head to the live petting zoo right at 9:00 AM. It's much quieter then, and your toddler will have better access to the animals before the older kids finish their breakfast.

After the Hunt: Memorial Park is one of Claremont's best playgrounds too — check out our Playground Guide for the full breakdown of every park in town.

Ages 3–5

For the “Prime” Hunters

This is the most "high-stakes" age group. They are old enough to be excited but small enough to get lost in the shuffle.

The Perimeter Strategy: Position yourself on the edge of the hunt zone early. Hold your child's hand until the signal goes off to ensure they don't get a "false start."

Focus on Fun, Not Volume: Remind them that finding just two or three eggs is a win. The real goal is the interactive booths and the photos with the Spring Bunnies.

Ages 6–12

For the Big Kids

This group is purely focused on one thing: The Golden Egg.

The Golden Hunt: There are special golden eggs hidden in the field that can be redeemed for larger prizes. Encourage your older kids to look near the base of the park's historic trees or tucked into the perimeter shrubbery.

Survival Tips: The “Pro” Checklist

Parking Hack

Memorial Park's main lot will be full by 8:30 AM. Look for street parking on 8th Street or Harvard Avenue north of the park. Better yet, if you live in the Village, walk or bike in to avoid the Indian Hill Boulevard traffic.

The Gear

Bring a basket that is deep — shallow plates or small bags often lead to "egg spill" during the 10:45 AM dash.
