The Insider's Guide to the 2026 Native Plant Festival

Workshops, native plant sales, food trucks, and free admission — March 28, 2026

In partnership with California Botanic Garden
When Saturday, March 28, 2026
Time 10:00 AM – 3:00 PM
Where California Botanic Garden
Admission Free ($5 suggested donation)
Get Free Tickets

Every spring, California Botanic Garden throws open its gates for the biggest native plant celebration in the region. Across 86 acres of winding trails, California wildflowers, and ancient oaks, the Native Plant Festival brings together plant nerds, families, hikers, and anyone who wants to see what Southern California looked like before the freeways. Here's everything you need to know to make the most of it.

Free Admission — Reserve Ahead

The festival's headline draw: free admission to the entire garden (with a $5 suggested donation to support CalBG's education and conservation programs). Normally $15 for adults, this is your chance to explore all 86 acres without the ticket price. Advance, timed ticket reservations are highly recommended — walk-up tickets are available subject to capacity, but book early as spots fill up.

Reserve Your Free Ticket

Timed-entry slots are available on CalBG's website. Each ticket covers one person for a specific entry window. Pro tip: arriving right at 10:00 AM gives you the smallest crowds and the best light for photos.

The Library Hack: Discover & Go

Insider Tip: Free Passes Year-Round

LA County Library and LA Public Library cardholders can reserve free CalBG passes through the Discover & Go program — not just during the festival, but any time of year. If you have a library card, check for availability before you buy regular admission. It's one of the best-kept secrets in the Inland Empire.

What to Do at the Festival

This isn't just a walk through the garden — the festival packs a full day of programming. Here's what's on the schedule:

Workshops & Tours

Native plant landscaping workshops, Production Nursery tours (with bilingual Spanish/English options), Herbarium tours, and a seedbanking conservation talk. There are also panel discussions on plant-people connections and an ethnobotany presentation on White Sage.

Kids & Family

Storytelling and craft stations, face painting and henna, plus hands-on activities throughout the garden. Plenty to keep kids engaged while you browse the plants.

Exhibitors & Artisan Market

30+ nonprofit and agency exhibitor booths, native plant landscaping companies, and an artisan market with nature-themed vendors. Raffle prizes, too.

Live Music

Live musical performances throughout the day — a nice backdrop while you wander the trails.

The Nursery Sale

Late March is prime planting season, and CalBG's Grow Native Nursery runs a major sale during the festival — this is where you can take the garden home with you.

CalBG Grow Native Nursery

The on-site nursery stocks hundreds of California native species, many propagated from the garden's own collections. Staff and volunteers are on hand to help you pick the right plants for your soil, sun, and water situation. Expect to leave with at least one flat you didn't plan on buying.

Native Plant Cheat Sheet

Not sure what you're looking at? Here are some of the stars you'll spot on the trails during festival day.

Plant Why It's Great Best Spot
California Poppy The state flower. Blazing orange carpets in full sun — peak bloom is mid-April. Mesa Garden & hillside trails
Matilija Poppy Huge white "fried egg" flowers on 8-foot stalks. Just starting to bloom in most spots, but there's a good bloom already underway. Northern California Plant Communities section
Ceanothus Clouds of deep blue-purple flowers. A hummingbird magnet and drought champion. Mesa Gardens
Manzanita Smooth red bark, tiny urn-shaped flowers. Looks like sculpture in winter. Woodland trails
Coast Live Oak The backbone of California's native landscape. Ancient specimens throughout the garden. Oak woodland loop
Dudleya (Liveforever) Silvery rosettes that thrive on neglect. The ultimate low-water succulent. Mesa Gardens & SoCal Gardens

Food & Drink

Food trucks set up near the main entrance during the festival. Here's the 2026 lineup:

Word of Mouth Vegan Food Truck

Fully vegan menu — a great option for plant-based eaters at a plant-based festival.

Crepes Bonaparte

Sweet and savory crepes. Grab one and eat it under the oaks.

Marigold Coffee Collective

Your caffeine fix for the day — because five hours of plant shopping requires fuel.

Pro Tips for a Smooth Visit
  • Parking: Free parking is available, but the garden lot fills up fast on festival day. Consider carpooling or taking public transit — Foothill Transit Rt. 188 and Metrolink both get you close.
  • Shoes: Wear closed-toe shoes with grip. The trails are dirt and gravel, and some paths are uneven.
  • Sun: There's limited shade on the mesa trails. Bring a hat, sunscreen, and water — late March in Claremont can be warm.
  • Timing: Arrive right at 10:00 AM for the smallest crowds and best energy. The festival wraps at 3:00 PM, so plan accordingly.
  • Nursery strategy: Hit the nursery first if you're serious about buying. The best specimens go early, especially California fuchsia and salvias.
  • Bring cash: Some vendors and the plant sale may be cash-preferred.
