Claremont's 2026 Fourth of July Playbook

Pancakes at sunrise, a parade down Indian Hill, and Pyrospectaculars over the foothills — here's the whole day.

Date Saturday, July 4, 2026
Morning Memorial Park
Evening Strehle Track · $15
Get Tickets & Info →

There is nothing quite like Independence Day in the 91711. Claremont truly pulls out all the stops, turning the entire day into a town-wide celebration that runs from a 7 a.m. pancake stack under the shade trees of Memorial Park all the way through to a 9 p.m. fireworks finale at Pomona College. This year, the City's official theme is USA! United We Shine! — here's your local's breakdown of the full day for Saturday, July 4, 2026.

🥞The Morning: Heritage, Races & Festival

The festivities kick off early at Memorial Park (840 N. Indian Hill Blvd) with traditions for early-morning runners, hungry families, and kids of every age. Bring a blanket, claim some shade, and pace yourself — this is a marathon, not a sprint.

Memorial Park Morning Schedule

All events at 840 N. Indian Hill Blvd

  • 7:00 – 10:00 AMKiwanis Club Pancake Breakfast — a hot stack served right in the park.
  • 7:30 AM1K Kids Fun Run — a festive dash for the little ones, hosted by the Sunrise Rotary Club.
  • 8:00 AMFreedom 5K — the annual community 5K run/walk through Claremont's tree-lined streets, also hosted by the Sunrise Rotary Club.
  • 10:00 AMFlag Raising Ceremony — the patriotic kickoff to the main festival.
  • 10:00 AM – 1:00 PMFourth of July Festival — live entertainment, community booths, local food concessions, and free children's activities.
  • 10:15 – 11:30 AMT. Willard Hunter Speakers Corner — a Claremont tradition where local voices and community orators share their insights.
  • 12:00 PMHonoree Recognition — celebrating this year's grand marshals and community leaders.

🎺The Afternoon: The Community Parade

Grab your lawn chairs and stake out a shady spot early. The beloved annual community parade features decorated floats, marching bands, youth sports teams, and neighborhood groups celebrating together.

Parade — 1:00 PM Sharp

~1 mile, Memorial Park → Larkin Park

  • StartSteps off from Memorial Park
  • RouteSouth down Indian Hill Boulevard, then west on Harrison Avenue
  • FinishWraps up at Larkin Park

🎆The Evening: Concert, BBQ & Fireworks

When the sun starts to drop, the celebration moves down to Pomona College's Strehle Track (522 N. Mills Ave) for live music, dinner on the grass, and a world-class fireworks display engineered by Pyrospectaculars — the same crew behind some of Southern California's biggest shows.

Strehle Track Evening Schedule

522 N. Mills Ave · Pomona College

  • 6:30 PMGates Open — arrive early to claim a spot on the grass. Bags and coolers are subject to search at the gate.
  • 6:30 – 9:00 PMKiwanis Club BBQ — skip cooking dinner; classic fresh-off-the-grill BBQ inside the stadium.
  • 6:45 – 8:45 PMLive Concert with Pop Vinyl — local-favorite pop & dance band turning the track into a party.
  • 9:00 PMFireworks Show by Pyrospectaculars — the main event, lighting up the foothill skies.

🎫Ticket Information

  • Cost: $15 per ticket. Children 3 and under are free.
  • Online: Buy through the City of Claremont's official 4th of July page.
  • In person: Alexander Hughes Community Center, 1700 Danbury Road.
  • Heads up: The evening event frequently sells out — locals recommend buying ahead rather than counting on the door.

📝Stadium Rules: What to Know Before the Gate

To protect the track and keep the evening safe for everyone, Pomona College and the City enforce a strict items policy at the gate. Here's the short version.

Leave at Home

  • Alcohol
  • Glass bottles
  • Weapons
  • Animals (service animals welcome)
  • Canopies & tents
  • Smoking & vaping
  • E-bikes, scooters & skateboards

Bring Along

  • Lawn chairs & blankets (no fixed seating)
  • Outside food
  • Non-alcoholic drinks
  • Coolers (subject to search)
  • Strollers & wagons
  • Sunscreen & a layer for after dark

Enjoy the community, stay hydrated in the sun, and have a fantastic, safe Fourth of July, Claremont.
