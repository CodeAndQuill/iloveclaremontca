The Secret History of Your Claremont House

Every house in Claremont has a story — and for 50 years, Claremont Heritage has been the keeper of the keys.

Celebrating the 50th Anniversary of Claremont Heritage (1976–2026)
Organization Claremont Heritage
Founded 1976 — 50 Years
Headquarters The Garner House, Memorial Park
Visit Claremont Heritage

If you've ever wondered who walked your hallways in 1920 or what your lot looked like when the Village was still mostly citrus groves, you don't have to guess. This year, as we celebrate the 50th Anniversary of Claremont Heritage (1976–2026), there has never been a better time to uncover the legacy of your own property. For five decades, this organization has stood as the guardian of our town's unique character. In a special golden anniversary collaboration, we're showing you how to use their deep archives to bridge the gap between your modern life and the "secret" history of your home.

1 Prepare Your "Keys"

You don't need a degree in history to get started, but you do need the right information to unlock the records. Before you head to the archives, gather these basics from the comfort of your couch:

The Essentials

Have your address and, if possible, your Assessor's Parcel Number (APN) ready. You can find your APN on your property tax bill or via the LA County Assessor's website.

Set a Goal

Are you looking for the name of the original owner? A vintage photo? Or are you planning a period-accurate renovation? Having a clear goal helps the archivists point you to the right folder.

2 The Garner House

Once you have your info, head to the heart of Memorial Park. The Garner House — a 1926 Spanish Colonial Revival masterpiece — serves as the headquarters for Claremont Heritage. This is where the paper trail begins. Their archives aren't just for professional historians; they are a community resource for every resident who wants to be a steward of their home's legacy.

Before You Go

The Heritage office is open by appointment only, Monday–Friday, 10am–3pm. Call 909.621.0848 or email info@ClaremontHeritage.org to arrange your archive visit before you head over.

3 Dig into the "House Files"

When you explore the archives, these are the primary resources you'll want to hunt down:

Historical Resource Surveys

Since 1980, Claremont has conducted extensive surveys of its historic neighborhoods. There's a high chance a "biography" of your house already exists, detailing its architectural style, build date, and original owner.

The Map Collection

Want to see what your street looked like in 1887? The archives hold large-format maps and early citrus grove plots that show exactly how the "City of Trees and PhDs" was carved out of the landscape.

City Directories

Before the internet, there were physical directories. These allow you to "reverse search" your address to find every family that called your house "home" over the last century.

The Photo Vault

Claremont Heritage maintains a massive collection of photographs. You might find a vintage shot of your street or even a candid photo of your front porch from the 1940s.

4 Consult the Experts

If you want to go beyond a basic search, the Heritage team offers specialized help for homeowners:

Architectural Consultations

If you're planning a restoration, they can help you identify original materials and era-appropriate details to keep your renovation authentic.

The Mills Act

Learn if your home qualifies for the Mills Act, which can provide significant property tax breaks in exchange for preserving the home's historic character. Claremont has participated in the program since 2000.

Celebrate 50 Years of Heritage

Beyond the archives, the best way to honor our town's history is to experience it. Claremont Heritage is marking its golden anniversary under the banner Preservation 50: Our Legacy, Our Future, and the calendar gives you plenty of chances to step inside the very stories you're researching:

The 2026 Home Tour — Sunday, October 11

The signature event of the anniversary year. Claremont Heritage's annual Home Tour opens the doors of some of the city's most significant architectural gems from 10am to 4pm — a rare chance to walk through the kind of history you've been reading about in the archives.

First-Saturday Village Walking Tours

A guided walk through the Village's historic core, offered the first Saturday of each month ($20/person, reservations required). Walking tours of the Claremont Colleges run on select fall Saturdays too.

At the Garner House This Summer

The Michael Graves Route 66 photo exhibition celebrates the Mother Road's 95th year in Claremont, and on August 1 the Treasury of Claremont Music takes over the Garner House courtyard for an evening concert (6–9pm, ticketed).

For specific dates and ticket information for these 50th-anniversary events, visit ClaremontHeritage.org or stop by the Garner House in Memorial Park.

Why It Matters

Uncovering your home's history does more than just satisfy curiosity — it connects you to the community. Knowing that a citrus packer once lived in your kitchen or that a Pomona College professor wrote a seminal book in your sunroom turns a house into a piece of Claremont's living history.

Pro Tip

Start by asking whether your house is on the Claremont Register of Structures of Historical and Architectural Merit — locals just call it "the Claremont Register." If it's already listed, the Heritage team likely has a folder full of documents waiting for you.
