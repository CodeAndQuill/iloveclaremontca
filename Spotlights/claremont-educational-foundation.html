Investing in the “City of Trees and PhDs”

How the Claremont Educational Foundation bridges the gap to protect art, music, and technology in our schools.

Featuring the Claremont Educational Foundation

Where Padua Hills Theatre

In a town that prides itself on academic excellence, the Claremont Educational Foundation (CEF) serves as the vital link between community generosity and classroom reality. While Claremont Unified School District (CUSD) is known for its high standards, state funding often falls short of covering the "extras" that define a well-rounded education. Since its inception, CEF has stepped in to fill that void, granting over $4 million to local schools to ensure that every student has access to a rich, creative, and tech-forward curriculum.

The Mission: Beyond the Basics

CEF operates on the belief that art, music, and technology aren't just "add-ons" — they are essential components of a modern education. Their impact is felt across every campus in the district through three primary funding streams:

Elementary Art & Music

CEF provides dedicated block grants to all seven elementary schools. This funding ensures that students receive consistent, professional instruction in the arts during their most formative years — programs that would otherwise be at risk due to budget constraints.

Secondary Technology Grants

At the middle and high school levels, the focus shifts to digital literacy. CEF funds have historically expanded access to essential tech tools at Claremont High School, El Roble Intermediate, and San Antonio High, providing everything from computer labs to specialized software for vocational training.

Teacher Innovation Grants

Perhaps the most personal of their initiatives, these grants allow individual educators to pitch "dream projects" for their classrooms. From STEM kits to inclusive playground equipment, these grants empower teachers to innovate without having to pay out of pocket.

The Big Event: Night of Comedy & Magic

Mark your calendars for Wednesday, April 15, 2026, for one of the foundation's signature fundraisers. Held at the historic Padua Hills Theatre, this event is designed to celebrate CUSD teachers and staff while raising the necessary funds for the upcoming school year's block grants.

Cocktails & Hors d'oeuvres

The evening kicks off at 5:30 PM with a chance for parents, local business leaders, and educators to connect in a relaxed setting.

World-Class Entertainment

Performances by professional comedians and magicians that turn a standard fundraiser into a high-energy community night.

Community Impact

All proceeds from ticket sales and sponsorships go directly back into the 91711 schools, specifically targeting the enrichment programs that make our district a destination for families.

How to Get Involved

Supporting CEF is a staple of the Claremont lifestyle, and there are several ways to contribute beyond attending the April gala:

The $365 Club

Many residents choose to donate "a dollar a day" ($365 per year). In exchange for this commitment, donors receive the iconic CEF yard sign — a familiar sight in Village and North Claremont neighborhoods that signals a direct investment in local youth.

Business Partnerships

Local business owners can sponsor events like the Chocolate Walk or the Annual Car Raffle, gaining visibility among local families while supporting public education.

Volunteer Opportunities

From serving on the board to helping coordinate the SLICE of Summer enrichment programs, CEF is powered by local volunteers who believe in the power of public schools.

Why It Matters

When you support CEF, you aren't just donating to a "school fund" — you are protecting the property values, the cultural heart, and the future workforce of Claremont. By ensuring our schools remain a lighthouse for art and technology, CEF helps maintain the "Claremont standard" that has defined our community for generations.

Pro Tip

If you're a CUSD parent, check your school's weekly newsletter for "Student Impact Grant" updates. You can often see exactly which new tools or instruments in your child's classroom were made possible by CEF donors.
