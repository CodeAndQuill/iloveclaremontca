How Safe is Your Neighborhood?

A guide to Claremont's new interactive crime map

Launched March 13, 2026
Source Claremont Police Dept.
Access Free · Mobile-Friendly
View the Crime Map →

Tip: If the map doesn't load right away, try refreshing the page.

Knowledge is power — and as of March 13, it's now interactive. If you've spent any time on local community boards lately, you know that "did anyone else hear that noise?" is practically the city's unofficial motto. But the Claremont Police Department has given us a way to trade neighborhood speculation for real-time data. With the launch of their new interactive crime tracker map, residents now have a transparent window into what's happening on their streets.

This isn't about fear-mongering; it's about being an informed neighbor. Here's how to navigate the new tool and what you should actually be looking for.

How to Use the Map

Step 1: Access the Interface

The map is hosted on the Claremont Citizen RIMS portal. It's mobile-friendly, but for a deep dive into your specific neighborhood, the desktop view offers the best perspective. When you first log in, you'll see a bird's-eye view of the city dotted with various incident icons.

Step 2: Filter for Context

Not every icon represents a "crime" in progress. To get a clear picture of your area, use the filtering tools located on the sidebar:

  • Incident Types: Toggle between categories like "Residential Burglary," "Vehicle Theft," or "Theft from Vehicle."
  • Date Ranges: The map defaults to the last 30 days, but you can adjust this to see long-term trends in your specific corner of the 91711.
  • Missing Persons & Stolen Vehicles: The map also tracks active cases, providing a broader look at CPD's current focus.

Step 3: Understand "Incident" vs. "Crime"

One thing to keep in mind: The map displays calls for service and reported incidents. This means you might see an icon for an "investigation" that doesn't necessarily result in an arrest or a confirmed crime.

Use the data as a trend-spotter. For example, if you notice a cluster of "Theft from Vehicle" icons near your local park, it's a great reminder to double-check your locks and remove valuables from your car.

Why It Matters: Beyond the Map

Claremont is a city that prides itself on being proactive. By launching this map, the CPD is inviting us to be part of the safety solution.

  • Transparency: It eliminates the "telephone game" of social media rumors.
  • Awareness: It helps Neighborhood Watch captains identify specific issues to discuss at meetings.
  • Prevention: Seeing where incidents occur allows residents to tighten up their home security in a targeted way.
Pro Tip

Don't let the icons overwhelm you. Most of the activity you see on the map is the result of a highly active police force and an engaged community that isn't afraid to "see something, say something."

How to Use This Data Responsibly

  • Check "Recent" vs. "Historical" data before posting to social media.
  • Use it for habit-building. If you see a rise in package thefts in your area, consider a secure drop-box or a neighbor-share program.
  • Remember the context. A single icon doesn't define a neighborhood; look at the month-over-month trends for a truer story.
